Features About

Intelligent Campus Navigation

ISU-GeoBot is a web-based campus navigation assistant for ISU Echague that integrates Random Forest predictive modeling and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to provide context-aware faculty availability information.

Launch Assistant

Core Research Innovations

Enhanced RAG Pipeline

Integrates a Random Forest classifier into the RAG pipeline to augment static document retrieval with real-time predictive analytics.

Status Masking

Ensures ethical privacy by transforming raw predictive outputs into generalized availability statuses, preventing invasive physical location tracking.

Context-Aware Navigation

Provides adaptive routing by fusing spatial campus data with real-time personnel schedules and institutional announcements.

About the Study

The Research Gap

Existing campus systems fail to fuse predictive classification with generative text retrieval. ISU-GeoBot bridges this by embedding a Random Forest classifier within a RAG architecture to provide dynamic, context-aware information grounded in verified institutional data.

Our Methodology

We employ a developmental research design, evaluating our architecture through the RAGAS framework (Context Precision, Recall, Faithfulness, and Answer Relevancy) and functional validation by selected faculty members acting as ground-truth validators.
